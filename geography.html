Our Geography

Originating as a Viking fishing village in the 10th century, it grew to become the economic core of Denmark. It’s located on the eastern shore of the island of Zealand, partly on the island of Amanger, as well as several natural and artificial islets. Copenhagen gets its name from Kaupmannahǫfn, meaning ‘merchants harbour’ as the city faces the Øresund, the strait the separates Denmark from Sweden.

From the sandy beaches, to the rolling hills, the Danish countryside has a wide range of geographic features.

Windmills! Windmills Everywhere!! With Denmark being a world leader in wind energy, wind farms are a large feature around Copenhagen. Once used for milling, wind is now Denmark's lagest source of energy.
